Art Deco

1910s-1950s

Art Deco, short for the French Arts Décoratifs, became the dominant artistic style of Europe and America up through the beginning of Worl War II. Devoted to the strong lines and geometric structures of the Machin Age, it was a strong departure from the sinuous, natural and elegant forms of its predecessor, Art Nouveau. It influenced everything from fashion to architecture, but the principal source of inspiration for the graphical choices behind the "ArtDeco style" of this website is its appearance in posters.

Punk Flyer and DIY Zine Aesthetic

1970s-1980s

Since the early 1930s, fan magazines or 'fanzines' have played a pivotal role in establishing a vibrant communication network within underground culture. These homemade publications have been instrumental in disseminating information and personal viewpoints on diverse subjects, ranging from music and football to anti-capitalism and thrift store shopping. However, it is within the subculture of punk music that the DIY ethos truly found its expression. In the late 1970s, A4-sized, stapled, and photocopied fanzines embraced 'do-it-yourself (DIY)' production techniques, employing cut-n-paste letterforms, collaged images, hand-scrawled and typewritten texts. This amalgamation resulted in a distinctive graphic design aesthetic, creating a recognizable visual style affiliated with punk fanzines.
